# 3D Binary Cube Isomorphic Model of the I Ching

## Core Claim

The complete structure of the I Ching can be represented as a 3-dimensional binary cube.

Mapping:

8 cube vertices  -> 8 trigrams
56 directed edges -> 56 transformation hexagrams
64 vertices+edges -> 64 hexagrams
384 line states -> 64 × 6 yao

This model treats the I Ching as a geometric information structure rather than a symbolic divination system.

## Definitions

Vertex:
A binary state represented by a 3-bit vector.

Trigram:
A mapping from a 3-bit vector to one of the eight fundamental trigrams.

Directed Edge:
A transition between two trigram states.

Hexagram:
A state-transition object consisting of either:
- a stable vertex
- a directed edge

Binary Cube:
The graph G=(V,E)
where:
|V| = 8
|E| = 56

# 3D Binary Cube and the I Ching

## Abstract

This project proposes a geometric ontology of the I Ching (Yijing) based on a three-dimensional binary cube.

The model demonstrates that:

- The 8 vertices of a cube correspond to the 8 fundamental trigrams.
- The 56 directed edges correspond to the 56 transformation hexagrams.
- Vertices and edges together form a complete 64-hexagram structure.
- The 384 Yao (line states) emerge naturally from the 64 hexagrams × 6 lines framework.

The model further suggests that traditional Chinese cosmological structures, including the Earlier Heaven Bagua, Later Heaven Bagua, Hetu, Luoshu, and Flying Star systems, can be interpreted as projections, traversals, or unfoldings of the same underlying binary-cube geometry.

Keywords: I Ching, Yijing, Binary Cube, Geometry, Ontology, Cosmology, Information Structure, Eight Trigrams, Hexagrams.
